Meet Chris Dyson

Chris leads training and development at Omega Training, and he has earned that role the hard way, through years on the floor with young people, not just behind a desk.

His path into training

From the front line to the training room

Chris started out working directly with children and young people, coaching, mentoring and helping them build the communication skills, personal goals and confidence to move forward in their lives.

From there, he moved into residential care leadership, managing people and processes across multiple sites: recruitment, budgets, rotas and the day to day reality of keeping a staff team strong. He has supported managers through Regulation 44 and 45 inspections and worked closely with Ofsted, so he understands compliance from the inside, not just from the guidance documents.

That experience shapes how he trains. Chris designs every session to be practical enough to use on shift the same day, and meaningful enough to change how people think about their role. He is a firm believer in creating a homely environment where young people can reach their potential, and he expects the same warmth and consistency from the staff he trains.

Training should have value in the room, and value once everyone has left it

Chris Dyson, Training and Development Lead
